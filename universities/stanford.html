Stanford University

Stanford University, founded in 1885 by Leland and Jane Stanford in Stanford,
California, stands as a leading private research institution. Its motto,
Die Luft der Freiheit weht ("The wind of freedom blows"), reflects its commitment
to academic freedom and innovation. As of August 1, 2024, the university is led
by its 13th president, Jonathan Levin, a distinguished economist and former dean of
the Graduate School of Business. Stanford's academic excellence spans disciplines
such as engineering, computer science, business, law, medicine, and sustainability.
The university enrolls approximately 16,000 students and boasts a substantial
endowment of $37.6 billion as of August 31, 2024.
