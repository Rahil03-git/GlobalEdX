Australian National University

Established in 1946 by an Act of the Federal Parliament, the Australian National
University (ANU) is Australia's only national university, located in Canberra. Its
motto, Naturam Primum Cognoscere Rerum ("First, to learn the nature of things"),
reflects its commitment to research excellence. As of 2024, Distinguished Professor
Genevieve Bell serves as Vice-Chancellor, with The Hon Julie Bishop as Chancellor.
ANU is renowned for its strengths in public policy, international relations, and the
sciences. The university hosts approximately 17,380 students, including 10,252
undergraduates and 7,128 postgraduates. ANU's alumni include six Nobel laureates
and numerous influential figures in academia and public service.
